AI Use & Assurance

How WaterApps uses AI-assisted workflows in a controlled, accountable delivery model for enterprise and regulated environments.

Last updated: March 3, 2026

1. How We Use AI-Assisted Workflows

We use role-based AI assistance to support research, drafting, engineering operations, QA checks, and release preparation.

AI tools accelerate execution, but they do not replace accountable engineering decisions or client governance obligations.

2. Human Accountability and Approval

  • Human engineers remain accountable for architecture, code, and production outcomes.
  • Client-facing outputs and production-impacting actions require human review and approval.
  • Final release decisions follow change-control and delivery governance practices.

3. Data Handling Controls

  • Client secrets, credentials, and sensitive regulated data are not submitted to external AI tools without explicit client approval.
  • We apply least-privilege and environment separation practices in delivery workflows.
  • Where required, we operate with client-specific restrictions on tool usage and data boundaries.

4. Quality and Release Controls

  • Outputs are validated through review, testing, and release readiness checks.
  • Production changes follow traceability expectations and deployment governance.
  • Operational workflows are designed for auditability, rollback, and incident response.

5. Client Options

If your procurement, risk, or security policy requires stricter controls, we can run designated scopes in a restricted mode.

  • No external AI tools for specified workstreams.
  • Client-approved tool list and data classifications.
  • Documented controls in SOW or engagement addendum.

We can provide AI-use assurance wording, delivery controls, and engagement-specific handling boundaries as part of procurement and risk review.
