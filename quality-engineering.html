Why Off-the-Shelf QE Platforms Fall Short in Regulated Environments

They assume modern stacks

Tricentis and Harness are designed for cloud-native microservices. Banks and telcos run hybrid estates — mainframes, packaged software, legacy APIs — that require a different testing approach and tool selection entirely.

They ignore compliance overhead

APRA CPS 234, Essential 8, and CAB processes aren't optional. Generic QE platforms generate metrics dashboards, not regulatory evidence trails. Someone still has to do that translation work — usually manually.

They don't know your regulator

Australian financial services, government, and telco have local regulatory nuance that global vendors don't embed into their methodology. WaterApps operates in this context — it's not an add-on.
