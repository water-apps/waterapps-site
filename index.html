Real Case Example

AKS non-prod scaling bottleneck resolved in 2 focused days

A banking platform team had autoscaling enabled but workloads still were not landing in the right scale zone. The issue was not raw capacity. It was the interaction between admission control and a missing scheduling value.

Root Cause

Admission control policy plus a missing config value affecting zone-based scheduling.

What Changed

Cluster autoscaler settings, expander profile, supporting config, and workload policy alignment were reviewed and corrected.

Outcome

Scaling behavior became predictable again, with clearer documentation for future tests and handovers.

Why this matters

This is the pattern WaterApps sees often in enterprise Kubernetes: tools are present, but policies, node-pool intent, and real scheduling behavior are not aligned.

How We Work

A structured delivery model for platform teams that need clear decisions, controlled implementation, and documentation strong enough to survive handover.

1

Understand the Stack

Start with the real platform, not assumptions. We review current architecture, constraints, policies, and where delivery is actually breaking down.

  • Problem framing + business impact
  • Environment and policy review
  • Success criteria before changes begin
2

Design the Path

Turn analysis into a practical design: target architecture, rollout sequence, control points, and the fastest safe path to value.

  • Architecture options + trade-offs
  • Quick wins and sequencing
  • Owner-ready roadmap
3

Implement the Fix

Resolve the bottleneck properly. That can mean infrastructure, policies, deployment flow, observability, or the operating model around them.

  • Infrastructure + pipelines + guardrails
  • Policy and scheduling validation
  • Testing in the right environment
4

Document and Enable

Leave behind something repeatable. Clear documentation and handover matter as much as the implementation itself.

  • Runbooks and architecture notes
  • Operational handover
  • Lessons captured for future runs

Do you work with existing toolchains?

Yes. Most enterprise environments have multiple CI/CD tools and legacy constraints. WaterApps focuses on improving flow and compliance without forcing a full rebuild.

Can you help with audit readiness?

That's a core strength. We design traceability, evidence capture, and control enforcement into delivery pipelines—so audit becomes "export and explain," not panic.

What does a typical first engagement look like?

Usually a short discovery + assessment, then phased delivery. You'll get a clear plan, quick wins early, and a roadmap for sustained improvement. Most clients see value in weeks, not months.

Do you work with government agencies?

Yes—WaterApps is registered on AusTender and NSW BuyICT. We understand government procurement, security requirements, and can scale through trusted subcontractors when needed.

Can you scale for larger engagements?

Absolutely. WaterApps maintains a pool of vetted DevOps, cloud, and AI engineers who can be brought in under a master subcontractor agreement. You get specialist depth without the overhead.
